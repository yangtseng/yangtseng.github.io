§ 01 — Research

Biology speaks in many layers. I try to listen to all of them.

Gene expression. Chromatin. Metabolites. Each tells part of the story of a disease; none alone is the whole. I currently work across three layers — scRNA-seq, scATAC-seq, and mass-spectrometry imaging — building methods that fuse them into a coherent view of how disease unfolds. The framework is designed to extend as new modalities come online.

My doctoral work sits between two laboratories: Fan Lam's group on biomedical imaging and machine learning, and Jonathan V. Sweedler's group on single-cell mass spectrometry.

Build models that don't just predict —
they explain what they see.
